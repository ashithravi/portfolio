Case Study 01 — Featured · Internal Tool

Zoom Access Board — a single source of truth for access, approvals, and reviews.

Centralized dashboard for meeting notes, action items, approvals, and access workflows — backed by automated routing, provisioning, and scheduled license reviews.

Internal Tool Access Mgmt N8N Automation Workflow

Context

Zoom-related operations — license assignment, access approvals, recording retention, and periodic reviews — were handled across IT tickets, Slack threads, email approvals, and ad-hoc spreadsheets. Different teams had partial visibility, and audits were difficult to assemble after the fact.

Problem

Routine requests took longer than they should. Approvers were unclear, ownership was distributed, and there was no single record of who had access to what or why. License waste was hard to track, and quarterly reviews relied on manual reconciliation.

Key Insight

The pain wasn't the absence of tools — every step already existed somewhere. The pain was that no single surface owned the request, the approval, and the audit trail together. Centralising the workflow mattered more than building new functionality.

Solutions

A single board for all Zoom-related requests, with automation handling the predictable parts.
  • Unified intake covering license requests, host upgrades, recording access, and meeting notes follow-ups.
  • Rule-based routing to the right approver based on team, cost, and risk level.
  • N8N workflows for provisioning, deprovisioning, and scheduled license reviews.
  • Audit timeline on every request — who asked, who approved, what changed, when.

Trade-offs

Chose to wrap existing systems with automation rather than replace them — faster to ship, but introduced a dependency on third-party APIs staying stable. Kept the UI deliberately plain so the focus stayed on workflow correctness rather than visual polish in v1.

Impact

Routine requests resolved in minutes instead of days. Quarterly license reviews shifted from a multi-day exercise to a scheduled job with a human checkpoint. Every access change became traceable, which made audits straightforward.

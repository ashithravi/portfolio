Case Study 02 — SamMegh Technologies

Streamlining corporate travel across booking, approvals, and payments.

Bringing transparency and structure to a fragmented enterprise travel workflow.

B2B SaaS Travel Workflow Payments

Context

Enterprises were managing corporate travel across multiple vendor platforms, with approvals handled through email and reconciliation done manually. That left workflows fragmented and gave both users and administrators only partial visibility.

Problem

Two issues drove most of the friction. Users abandoned bookings when pricing shifted between search and checkout. Approvals stalled because there was no structured workflow — finance only saw committed spend after the fact.

Key Insight

The primary issue wasn't a lack of functionality, but a lack of transparency and predictability — in pricing, in approval status, and in committed spend.

Solutions

The focus was on improving system clarity and reducing uncertainty:
  • Introduced fare breakdowns using Google Maps data so the price felt anchored, not estimated.
  • Replaced email-based approvals with rule-based workflows and real-time status tracking.
  • Integrated Razorpay for payment reliability and cleaner settlement.
  • Simplified the booking flow by trimming unnecessary steps and inputs.

Trade-offs

Deferred recommendation systems because data reliability wasn't there yet — predictability had to come first. Chose a single payment gateway to keep implementation and debugging tight, with a plan to add redundancy as volume grew.

Impact

Within one quarter, completed bookings increased by ~30%, drop-offs at the pricing stage reduced by ~20%, and approval turnaround time improved by ~40%. The system became more predictable for users and easier to manage for administrators.
